# Brand Guidelines

The BS Engineering visual identity should feel calm, technical and trustworthy.

It should not look like a loud personal brand. It should look like the work of someone who values clarity, structure and good documentation.

## Style direction

The style should be:

- minimal,
- technical,
- clean,
- structured,
- calm,
- evidence-based,
- human rather than corporate.

## Color palette

| Token | Hex | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | `#0F172A` | Main text and dark surfaces |
| Accent | `#2563EB` | Links, highlights and key accents |
| Secondary | `#475569` | Secondary text |
| Muted | `#64748B` | Supporting text |
| Background | `#FFFFFF` | Main document background |
| Soft background | `#F8FAFC` | Cards and subtle sections |
| Line | `#E2E8F0` | Dividers and borders |

## Typography

Primary resume typography: Roboto.

Design system and portfolio typography: Inter or Roboto depending on medium.

The priority is readability. A document that looks modern but is tiring to read is not good design.

## Design principles

- Use white space intentionally.
- Avoid decoration that does not support meaning.
- Use icons only when they improve scanning.
- Keep contrast high.
- Keep section hierarchy consistent.
- Make documents readable before making them visually impressive.
- Let the content breathe.

## Tone and feeling

BS Engineering should feel professional, but not cold.

The writing should be clear, honest and grounded. The design should support that tone: no unnecessary noise, no inflated visuals, no fake complexity.

## Document principle

Design cannot replace substance.

The role of design is to make strong, credible content easier to understand and remember.
